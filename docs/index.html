Digital Health Innovation for Personalised Medicine (DHIPM)

Project Overview

We are leading a multi-phase initiative to bring personalised medicine and digital health into Ghana’s healthcare system. Our goal is to empower both patients and clinicians to make better, more informed treatment choices.

We use N-of-1 trials—a special type of research where each patient tries different treatment options, one after the other, and tracks their own results. This way, patients and their doctors can clearly see which treatment works best for them as individuals, rather than relying only on general guidelines.

Our initiative responds to the urgent need for responsive, person-centred healthcare in Ghana, particularly for chronic conditions like hypertension. With tools such as StudyMe and StudyU, we aim to co-design integrated care pathways that respect both biomedical and traditional healing approaches.

Project Phases

  • ✓ Phase I – Digital Health Landscape & Scoping Review: Nationwide scan showed 60% mobile health activity, but under 8% engagement with personalised models. Gaps identified in infrastructure and integrative care awareness.
  • ✓ Phase II – Public & Clinician Engagement + Pilot Tools: Surveyed 487 public users and 32 clinicians across all 16 regions. StudyMe piloted with 67% active users tracking outcomes like sleep and pain. Only 2 clinicians tested StudyU and called for broader clinician education, support systems, and training.
  • 🎓 Phase III – Integrated Digital N-of-1 Trials for Hypertension: Focused on Ghana's top chronic condition, this phase pilots patient-led hypertension care models combining herbal and biomedical approaches in the Eastern Region.

Phase III: Personalised Hypertension Management through Digital N-of-1 Trials in Ghana

Focus: This phase tackles hypertension—affecting 1 in 3 Ghanaians—by using personalised, digital N-of-1 trials to help patients and clinicians evaluate herbal and biomedical treatment paths.

Study Setting: Eastern Region with Centre for Plant Medicine Research (CPMR), Mampong

Key Research Components
  1. Understanding Stakeholder Perspectives.
    Explore the attitudes, beliefs, and lived experiences of patients and clinicians regarding integrative approaches to hypertension care. Using qualitative methods (interviews, focus groups), the study will document the use and perspectives on both conventional treatments and on holistic practices (e.g., herbal remedies, diet, physical activity), perceived effectiveness, concerns, and openness to integrating both treatments.
    Objective: Identify commonly used conventional and holistic therapies and assess stakeholder views on their integration.
  2. Co-Designing Personalised Digital Trial Protocols
    Participatory co-design workshops with clinicians and patients will develop integrative N-of-1 trial protocols. These will compare conventional and integrative approaches, accommodating personalised treatment paths. The StudyU web platform (hosted by HPI) and the StudyU app will be adapted to local needs, ensuring user-friendly interfaces, outcome tracking, and real-time adjustments based on feedback.
    Objective: Design and test a culturally tailored, patient-centred digital trial system for integrative hypertension care.
  3. Piloting the Integrative Digital Trials
    Pilot the co-designed N-of-1 trials with 50 hypertensive patients. Participants will alternate between treatment options, assessing effectiveness via blood pressure(BP) monitoring using provided digital BP monitor and self-reported outcomes. Mixed-methods evaluation will assess feasibility, clinical impact, adherence, and satisfaction.
    Objective: Evaluate health outcomes, feasibility, adherence, and patient experiences within the digital framework.
  4. Developing an Ethical and Operational Framework
    Synthesize findings to create a comprehensive ethical and operational framework for scaling digital trials in low-resource settings, addressing consent, data governance, infrastructure readiness, clinician support, and policy alignment.
    Objective: Establish a practical framework for implementation and scale-up.
Stakeholders Engaged
  • Clinicians: doctors, nurses, pharmacists, dietitians
  • Traditional medicine experts: herbalists, CPMR researchers
  • Community members: patients, caregivers
  • Policy actors: Ghana Health Service, Ministry of Health
Expected Outcomes
  • Evidence-based care plans tailored to individuals
  • Five peer-reviewed publications and training materials
  • Blueprint for national scale-up
  • Capacity-building in digital tools and participatory research
Dissemination & Sustainability
  • Journals, stakeholder policy briefs, and conferences
  • Teaching toolkits and institutional partnerships
  • Scalable digital infrastructure for broader chronic disease care

Why Support This Project?

  • Innovation: Integrates biomedical and traditional therapies in ethical trials.
  • Capacity Building: Empowers local research with global relevance.
  • Transparency: Regular updates and financial tracking provided.
